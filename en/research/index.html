Research

Professor in the Department of Political Science at UFMG. He was a Postdoctoral Researcher at FGV-CEPESP, with a FAPESP scholarship. He holds a PhD in Political Science from USP (2021), with a doctoral scholarship from CNPq. During his PhD, he was a Visiting Fellow in the Department of Government at Harvard University (09/2019–02/2020), funded by a Capes-PrInt scholarship. He holds a MA in Political Science (2017), with a CNPq scholarship, and a BA in Law (2014) from UFMG.

He is an associated researcher with the following research groups and networks:

  • Center for Legislative Studies (CEL-UFMG)
  • Center of Politics and Economics in the Public Sector (FGV-CEPESP)
  • Study Group on Local Politics (NEPOL-UFJF) (NEPOL-UFJF)
  • Red de Estudios sobre Política Subnacional en América Latina (REPSAL)
  • Laboratório de Monitoramento e Avaliação de Políticas e Eleições (MAPE, IESP-UERJ)

Since 2025, he has co-coordinated the Legislab, a laboratory dedicated to legislative innovation and the improvement of legal quality, a collaborative project between the Law School and the Department of Political Science at UFMG.

He has professional experience in new technologies for data analysis and production, with expertise in political-electoral, socioeconomic, and social media data.

He served as a technical adviser at Fundação Seade (São Paulo State Statistical Bureau), developing projects using new technologies to produce and analyze data; as a data coordinator and analyst on Volt Data Lab’s and Núcleo Jornalismo’s projects; and as a data scientist on G1, where he produced news stories about the Brazilian general elections in 2018.

Research interests:
